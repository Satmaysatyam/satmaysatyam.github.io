Giving Back — How to not get a bad PI

This is the most useful student-facing page. Keep it honest, practical, and organized into clear sections.

Student mentorship
The interview phase
Ask about authorship, expected hours, equipment support, data access, and what happens when field conditions delay work.
Aligning expectations
Clarify timelines, communication style, and what success looks like in the first 3, 6, and 12 months.
Mentorship styles
Match your working style to the PI: micro-managed, hands-off, or balanced. Not every style suits every student.
Talk to the lab
Privately contact current and former students. Their answers are often more revealing than any interview question.
Red flags
Vague project ownership, unclear funding, no written expectations, and dismissive answers to basic questions.
