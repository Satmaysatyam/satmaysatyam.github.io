ABOUT ME

Satyam Gupta

Welcome! I am a behavioral ecologist from India, driven by a deep fascination with how animals navigate the complexities of collective life. My research bridges sociality, animal communication, and movement ecology to understand how groups coordinate, make decisions, and thrive in dynamic environments. Currently, I am a DAAD Fellow and PhD researcher at the DPZ Leibniz Institute for Primate Research in Göttingen, Germany. My doctoral research shifts focus to primates, where I investigate the fundamental drivers and mechanisms of collective movement in ring-tailed lemurs (Lemur catta). By studying how these highly social primates communicate and move as a cohesive unit, I aim to shed light on the evolutionary underpinnings of group coordination.
