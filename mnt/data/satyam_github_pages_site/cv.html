The Unseen Realities (Anti-CV)

The formal CV stays polished. The Anti-CV adds honesty, texture, and mentorship value by showing the unseen side of research life. It makes the profile human and useful for students who are looking for the right lab culture, not just the right project title.

Rejected Grants & Papers
For every accepted grant like the ATBC Seed Grant, there are several proposals that were rejected, requiring complete rethinking and rewriting. Rejection is the silent majority of academic life.
Failed Experiments
Hypotheses that failed, scripts in R and Python that broke unexpectedly during critical analysis, and data pipelines that had to be rebuilt from scratch after months of work.
Field Realities
The polished photos hide the torrential rain, freezing cold, exhausted batteries, broken gear, leeches, and the occasional silent, frustrating mornings in the forest where no data was collected.
Mentorship Lessons
Good science needs good boundaries: authorship, data ownership, field hours, and human dignity should be discussed early. Learning this often came through difficult experiences.
